"The Reunion"

From Shell of a Man

By Mario Anthony




EXT. LENNY'S NEIGHBORHOOD - CONTINUOUS

Faye walks fast, and ahead of Vincent. He rushes behind like a stressed out kindergartner after a flyaway kite.

VINCENT

You walk fast.

FAYE

Keep up.

She speeds past him again. Vincent feels down for the ride. Why not a challenge?

They continue their nighttime stroll in silence. Mystery, I guess. They end up at a nearby park.

Faye hastily plops on a bench. Vincent does the same, thoughtlessly.

She reaches in her purse, pulls out a tin cigarette box-- pulls out a rolled up blunt. She lights it. Vincent observes--

She looks at him, he looks away. And then--

FAYE

Are you stalking me?

VINCENT

Am I... what? No... no. I don't even know your name.

FAYE

Hm. Faye.

VINCENT

Vince. Vincent, but Vince.

They shake hands. How formal.

FAYE

So, Vince, who do you know from the party?

She passes him the blunt. He seems surprised.

VINCENT

Oh. Thanks, uh--

FAYE

You didn't think I was just gonna sit here and blow smoke into your face, did you?

VINCENT

Well, I was hoping not. But, some people do that.

FAYE

Some people blow weed smoke in your face and don't even pass it to you?

VINCENT

On occasion.

FAYE

Looks like it's time to surround yourself with some new people.

He passes it back. Nods in agreement.

VINCENT

I mostly know the guy who's house it is. Lenny. A couple other people I've seen here and there at parties. How 'bout you?

FAYE

Just Audrey.

VINCENT

Mmm. So, I thought we were going to get more green--

FAYE

You thought wrong. I never show up anywhere without my own.

VINCENT

But, what are you gonna bring back?

FAYE

I have more.

VINCENT

To sell?

FAYE

Well, this isn't a charity raffle.

...

FAYE (CONT'D)

What happened the other night?

VINCENT

That's a great question.

FAYE

What do you think happened?

VINCENT

You went to the bathroom and never came back.

FAYE

Oh... God.

VINCENT

What?

FAYE

Nothing.

VINCENT

No. What are you-- don't do that.

FAYE

You didn't take the hint?

VINCENT

What hint?

FAYE

Nevermind--

VINCENT

Oh no, come on, now you have to say it.

Pause. She looks him in the eye directly.

FAYE

I was waiting in the bathroom...

VINCENT

What?

FAYE

I put my thong inside my purse--

VINCENT

Wait... wait-- hold on a minute--

FAYE

I gave you a very specific look.

VINCENT

WHAT?!

FAYE

I feel so stupid.

VINCENT

You can't be serious. All you said was that you were going to the bathroom... with that... look-- Fuck.

FAYE

No. That's not the right word. Well, I guess it is.

VINCENT

I had no idea.

FAYE

Well, don't get any other ideas.

VINCENT

I didn't say anything.

FAYE

You want me to pass the blunt.

VINCENT

I mean, you're letting it burn, but it's your weed, I wasn't gonna--

FAYE

Why are you holding back so much? You shouldn't do that, say what's on your mind.

VINCENT

I've never really had a problem doing that.

FAYE

Coulda fooled me.

VINCENT

Well, for one, now I'm thinking about how we could've had mindblowing sex that night...

FAYE

Yeah, well you missed your chance.

She takes another hit. Vincent stares at her lips as they touch the blunt-- so feminine-- so angelic.

She passes it back to him.

VINCENT

You have beautiful lips.

FAYE

Okay...

VINCENT

Hey. I'm just exercising my ability to not hold back. As you suggested.

FAYE

And you thought complimenting my lips was the right first choice?

VINCENT

I thought that whatever it was, I probably shouldn't give it too much thought, otherwise it wouldn't be as authentic.

FAYE

I never mentioned not putting thought into what you say.

VINCENT

Yeah, but we're talking about how I feel, ya know? And what I seem to be holding back, according to you.

FAYE

I'm sure we're just scratching the surface.

VINCENT

I think the first step was me going up to you that night at Ozzy's.

FAYE

With your beer goggles on--

VINCENT

Are you seeing anyone?

FAYE

What are you seeing, loverboy?

VINCENT

Stars.

FAYE

You're weird.

VINCENT

I think you like it.

FAYE

I think you're wrong.

VINCENT

I think you think I'm cute. You put your thong in your purse for me.

FAYE

You're really not as cute as you think you are.

VINCENT

No, but I'm probably as cute as you think I am.

FAYE

Which isn't very much.



Mario Anthony is a 25-year old self-taught actor, filmmaker, musician, and now author from Chicago, IL. He completed the first draft of his debut screenplay at the age of 21, something he began when he was 18. That screenplay is what eventually became the inspiration behind the publishing of his first novel, Shell of a Man, which can be purchased on Amazon now. Mario plans on pursuing more in film and music in the coming years, in hopes to showcase more sides of himself as a creative. He is on Instagram under the name @m.anthonystein.
